STUDY SESSION

1.   Palo Alto Golf Course Re-configuration Resulting From the San
     Francisquito Flood Control Project.

James Keene, City Manager, stated that the San Francisquito Creek Joint
Powers Authority (JPA) had been working on the JPA Creek Capital Project to
deal with flooding issues. Staff had been researching options to realign the
golf course, but there were a number of policy issues which have significant
financial implications.

Len Matterman, Executive Director of the JPA, described the creek and its
course through Palo Alto. He stated the proposals submitted by the Santa
Clara Valley Water District and the JPA were designed to remove land
parcels from the flood plain. He noted a Study Session on JPA issues was
scheduled for the following week. He then described the area affected by
the Project using a map. He stated the impact of the Project was the
movement of the levy, and the mitigation was to maintain and improve the
golf course. He said the JPA was supporting the mitigations only. He
indicated the JPA had questions about possibly enhancing the golf course as
well.
